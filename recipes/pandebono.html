Pandebono

Description

My favorite recipe of them all, the delicious pandebono, is another wonderful Colombian delicacy created using masarepa, yuca flour, cheese, and an egg. Sometimes just called cheese bread, they can be eaten any time of the day, and go well with coffee. It is not hard to convince yourself to eat as many as possible in one sitting.

Tips before baking:

  • Use a blender to mix together the dough, which consists of the tapioca starch and precooked cornmeal. In another bowl, combine cheeses and the egg, adding the flour mixture and mixing well by hand until fully combined. If dough is too dry, add a tablespoon of milk.
  • While shaping balls of dough, use oil on your hands, as the dough mix will be very sticky.
  • While they can be baked immediately after dough mix has been added to the cheese and uncooked pandebonos have been rolled and formed, optimally they should be kept in the fridge to settle for at least 15 minutes before baking.
